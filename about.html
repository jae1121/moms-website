About

The M.O.M.S. Story

The M.O.M.S. Group was founded in June 2019 by Tina Ford, a Clairton resident, in the wake of the death of her son Armani Ford. Through her pain, a purpose was birthed. Tina formed the group to reach out and help support others who have unfortunately experienced the same tragedy.

Since its inception the M.O.M.S. Group has held several fundraisers to support the group participants in traveling to speaking engagements to spread awareness of the issue of violence and the rippling effect it has on the community.

Our Mission

We are different in our approach when it comes to how we help to heal. In addition to helping our moms grab onto hope again, we incorporate the community in our healing process to give back.

Moms in our group often host legacy projects, community-driven events such as fundraisers, to honor their lost loved ones. We have also put together an autobiographical drama titled The Mom-a-logues, where several members of the group speak on their loss to audiences.

With the support of M.O.M.S. Group, Rep. Austin Davis introduced legislation that will help provide additional time and space for families of victims to grieve without fear of becoming homeless and/or experiencing financial difficulty.
